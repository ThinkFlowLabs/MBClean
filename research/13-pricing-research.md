## Detailed Breakdown by Service

### 1. Commercial Deep Cleaning (One-Time Project)

**What the market charges:**
- National average: $0.05 - $0.20/sqft for routine commercial cleaning
- One-time deep cleaning: $0.15 - $0.80/sqft (2-4x routine pricing)
- Hourly rates: $25 - $50/hour per worker
- South Florida: aligns with or slightly exceeds national due to higher labor costs

**Pricing factors:**
- Size of facility (larger = lower per sqft)
- Condition / level of neglect
- Type of facility (medical costs more than standard office)
- Access and hours (after-hours premium)
- Scope: surfaces cleaned, equipment needed

**Sources**: HomeGuide ($0.08-$0.20 routine), Angi ($0.10-$0.20 routine), Perplexity research ($0.05-$0.80 deep clean in S.FL)

---

### 2. Commercial Floor Deep Cleaning

**What the market charges:**
- Basic floor cleaning: $0.07 - $0.15/sqft
- Deep floor cleaning (scrub + rinse + dry): $0.12 - $0.50/sqft
- Factors: floor type (VCT, concrete, hardwood), condition, buildup level

**Sources**: HouseCallPro ($0.07-$0.25/sqft commercial), Perplexity ($0.12-$0.50 for deep cleaning in S.FL)

---

### 3. Floor Stripping & Waxing

**What the market charges:**
- Wax only (no strip): $0.69 - $0.85/sqft (HomeWyse 2025)
- Full strip + wax: $1.69 - $2.13/sqft (HomeWyse 2025)
- VCT commercial strip + wax: $0.70 - $0.75/sqft (Thumbtack, high-traffic)
- Reddit/industry forums: $0.30 - $1.50/sqft depending on market
- Extra coats of wax: add $0.10 - $0.25/sqft per coat (HomeGuide)
- Average commercial job: $0.50 - $1.25/sqft

**Pricing factors:**
- Number of old wax layers to strip (more = more labor)
- Floor condition (damage, staining)
- Number of new wax coats desired (2-4 typical)
- Furniture moving required
- Square footage (volume discounts for large areas)
- VCT vs. other floor types

**MB Clean is already at $0.80 - $1.25/sqft** -- this is well within competitive range.

**Sources**: HomeWyse, HomeGuide, Thumbtack, Reddit r/maintenance, Janitorial Store

### 5. Commercial Carpet Cleaning

**What the market charges:**
- Commercial hot water extraction: $0.15 - $0.35/sqft (Big League Clean 2025)
- General commercial carpet: $0.20 - $0.50/sqft (HomeGuide 2026)
- Residential comparison: $0.25 - $0.90/sqft (higher per sqft, smaller jobs)
- Per room: $40 - $125/room (residential reference)
- HomeWyse 2026: $0.41 - $0.51/sqft

**Pricing factors:**
- Carpet type and fiber
- Stain severity / level of soiling
- Square footage (commercial = volume pricing)
- Cleaning method (hot water extraction vs. encapsulation vs. bonnet)
- Furniture moving required
- Pre-treatment needed

**Sources**: Big League Clean, HomeGuide, Angi, HomeWyse, HouseCallPro, Thumbtack

### 6. Commercial Pressure Washing

**What the market charges:**
- Commercial general: $0.08 - $0.30/sqft (Twisted Nozzle, Houston)
- Sidewalks: $0.15 - $0.30/sqft (Cutting Edge 2025)
- Driveways/parking lots: $0.35 - $0.77/sqft residential, lower for commercial
- Concrete cleaning: $0.42 - $0.52/sqft (HomeWyse 2026)
- Building exteriors: $0.15 - $0.40/sqft
- Flat rate examples: sidewalks $100-$200, small buildings $250-$500

**Pricing factors:**
- Surface type (concrete, brick, stucco, wood)
- Surface condition (oil stains, mold, algae -- common in S.FL humidity)
- Area size (volume discounts for parking lots)
- Height/accessibility (multi-story = more)
- Chemical treatment needed (mold removal, degreasing)
- Water source availability

**South Florida note**: Higher demand year-round due to humidity, mold, and algae growth. Properties need more frequent pressure washing than in drier climates.

**Sources**: HomeGuide, HomeWyse, Twisted Nozzle, Cutting Edge, InvoiceFly

### 7. Tile & Grout Cleaning

**What the market charges:**
- Basic tile & grout cleaning: $0.50 - $3.00/sqft (Angi, HomeAdvisor)
- HomeWyse 2025: $0.78 - $0.97/sqft
- Deep clean + seal: $1.50 - $6.00+/sqft (Networx 2026)
- Average project: $250 - $750 total (HomeGuide)
- Industry/commercial forums: ~$1.00/sqft standard
- National average project: ~$479 (Angi 2025)

**Pricing factors:**
- Tile type (ceramic, porcelain, natural stone)
- Grout condition (mildew, discoloration, damage)
- Sealing included or not
- Square footage
- Accessibility (commercial kitchens, restrooms)

**South Florida note**: High humidity = more mold/mildew in grout = often requires deeper restoration, justifying higher pricing.

**Sources**: Angi, HomeAdvisor, HomeGuide, HomeWyse, InchCalculator, Networx, TMF Forums

### 8. Marble & Terrazzo Polishing

**What the market charges:**
- Marble polishing: ~$2.00/sqft (Sentral Services)
- Terrazzo restoration: $1.00 - $2.00/sqft basic (HomeAdvisor)
- Terrazzo polishing (professional): $3.00 - $7.00/sqft (Sentral Services)
- Terrazzo full restoration (grinding + polishing): $5.00 - $12.00/sqft (Granite Heroes)
- Total project range: $200 - $2,500 depending on area size

**This is the HIGHEST-MARGIN service** -- specialized skill, less competition, premium pricing.

**Pricing factors:**
- Stone type (marble, terrazzo, travertine, granite)
- Current condition (scratches, etching, staining, dullness)
- Level of restoration needed (polish only vs. grind + polish)
- Square footage
- Sealing included or not
- Access/furniture removal

**South Florida note**: Terrazzo is EXTREMELY common in S.FL (many mid-century buildings, condos, lobbies). This is a huge local market. Marble lobbies in luxury condos and office buildings are everywhere. This is MB Clean's premium differentiator.

**Sources**: HomeAdvisor, Angi, Sentral Services, Granite Heroes, Craftsman Concrete

### 9. Commercial Air Duct Cleaning

**What the market charges:**
- Per vent: $25 - $50/vent (HomeGuide, Angi, HomeAdvisor, Fixr, Sunset Air)
- Per return vent: up to $75/return (larger, collects more debris)
- Per square foot: $0.15 - $0.40/sqft (This Old House 2026)
- Whole system (residential): $450 - $1,000 (HomeGuide)
- Commercial systems: $500 - $5,000+ depending on size
- Budget warning: offers under $300 are usually scams or partial cleanings

**Pricing factors:**
- Number of vents/returns
- Ductwork length and accessibility
- Level of contamination (mold, dust, debris)
- System size (tons of HVAC)
- Building height and duct access
- Mold remediation if needed (significant upcharge)

**South Florida note**: A/C runs year-round in S.FL = more dust/debris accumulation. Humidity creates mold risk in ducts. High demand for this service.

**Sources**: HomeGuide, Angi, HomeAdvisor, This Old House, Fixr, Sunset Air, PS Energy, HouseCallPro

### 10. Post-Construction Cleaning

**What the market charges:**
- Rough clean: $0.10 - $0.30/sqft (Bryea Plus 2026)
- Final clean: $0.25 - $0.60/sqft (HomeGuide 2026)
- Touch-up clean: $0.05 - $0.15/sqft
- Full post-construction (all phases): $0.15 - $0.80/sqft (HomeGuide, Ziva)
- Commercial projects average: $0.30 - $0.75/sqft for full final clean (Ziva 2026)
- National average: ~$0.25/sqft (Angi, HomeAdvisor)
- Flat rate example: ~$650 for average residential (Fixr)

**Pricing factors:**
- Phase of cleaning (rough, final, touch-up)
- Type of construction (new build vs. renovation)
- Debris volume and types (drywall dust, adhesive, paint)
- Building size and complexity
- Window cleaning included or not
- Timeline pressure (rush jobs cost more)
- Floor type (delicate floors need careful cleanup)

**South Florida note**: Construction boom in Miami-Dade = steady demand. High-rise condos and commercial developments provide large, recurring project opportunities from contractors.

**Sources**: HomeGuide, Angi, HomeAdvisor, Fixr, Ziva Cleaning, Bryea Plus, ServiceMaster, GetWeCycle

## Competitive Positioning: Pricing Strategy for MB Clean

### Why Show Pricing (When Competitors Don't)

From our national competitor analysis, **only 1 out of 12+ competitors** shows any pricing info on their website. This creates a massive trust and conversion opportunity:

1. **Searchers want pricing** -- "how much does [service] cost" keywords get thousands of monthly searches
2. **Showing ranges builds trust** -- visitors feel respected, not ambushed by sales calls
3. **Filters tire-kickers** -- people who can't afford the range won't waste your time
4. **SEO advantage** -- pricing content ranks for "cost of [service]" keywords
5. **LLM advantage** -- AI assistants love citing specific pricing data

### Recommended Display Format for Website

```
Starting at $X.XX - $Y.YY per sqft
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Final pricing depends on:
  ✓ Square footage of the area
  ✓ Current condition of surfaces
  ✓ Scope and complexity of the project
  ✓ Access and scheduling requirements

📞 Get a free on-site assessment for an accurate quote
```
