## 1. Optimal Site Architecture: Location Hubs + Service Sub-Pages

**Recommended approach**: Location Hub pages with Service Sub-Pages

### URL Structure
```
/ (Homepage)
/services/
    /services/commercial-floor-deep-cleaning/
    /services/stripping-and-waxing/
    /services/scrubbing-and-wax/
    /services/carpet-cleaning/
    /services/pressure-washing/
    /services/tile-grout-cleaning/
    /services/marble-terrazzo-polishing/
    /services/air-duct-cleaning/
    /services/post-construction-cleaning/
/areas/
    /areas/miami-dade/
        /areas/miami-dade/marble-polishing/
        /areas/miami-dade/floor-waxing/
        (etc for each service)
    /areas/broward/
        /areas/broward/marble-polishing/
        (etc)
    /areas/palm-beach/
        /areas/palm-beach/marble-polishing/
        (etc)
/blog/
/about/
/contact/
/reviews/
```

### Why This Beats Alternatives
- Service+Location combo pages (50+) risk thin content penalties
- Single service pages miss local keyword targeting
- Location hubs with sub-pages = best crawl budget, unique content, and UX

### Internal Linking Strategy
- Homepage links to all 3 county hubs and all service pages
- Service pages link down to county variants
- County hubs link to all service sub-pages within
- Blog posts link to relevant service+location pages
- Breadcrumb navigation on every page

## 2. Google Business Profile (GBP) Strategy

### Setup
- **Profile Type**: Service-Area Business (SAB) -- hide physical address
- **Primary Category**: "Commercial Cleaning Service"
- **Secondary Categories**: "Office Cleaning Service," "Janitorial Service," "Floor Cleaning Service," "Carpet Cleaning Service," "Stone Restoration Service"
- **Service Area**: Miami-Dade County, Broward County, Palm Beach County + key individual cities (up to 20)

### ONE listing only (not multiple)
Multiple listings require separate physical locations. Use one GBP with comprehensive service area coverage.

### Multi-County Posting Strategy (Weekly Rotation)
- Week 1: Miami-Dade focus
- Week 2: Broward focus
- Week 3: Palm Beach focus
- Week 4: Pan-South Florida offer or seasonal content

### Review Strategy
- Target 5-10 reviews/month
- Ask clients to mention specific city/county in review text
- Respond to ALL reviews within 24 hours
- Geographic diversity in reviews strengthens multi-county signals

## 3. Schema Markup Implementation

### Required Schema Types
1. **LocalBusiness** (Homepage) -- with areaServed for all 3 counties
2. **Organization** (Sitewide) -- brand identity
3. **Service** (Per service page) -- each service with provider info
4. **OfferCatalog** (Services overview) -- list all services
5. **FAQPage** (FAQ and service pages) -- target "People Also Ask"
6. **BreadcrumbList** (All interior pages) -- navigation hierarchy
7. **Review/AggregateRating** (Testimonials) -- star ratings

### Key Implementation Notes
- JSON-LD format only (Google preferred)
- Never expose physical address in schema for SABs
- Keep schema data synced with GBP data
- Per-area pages get specific areaServed schema

## 5. Link-Building Strategy

### Foundation: Directory Submissions (30+)
**National**: Google Business Profile, Yelp, BBB Florida, Angi, Thumbtack, HomeAdvisor, Bing Places, Apple Maps, Facebook, Manta, Nextdoor
**Florida-Specific**: FL Commercial Cleaning Directory, Estimatty Florida Cleaning, Greater Miami Chamber, Fort Lauderdale Chamber, Palm Beach Chamber, Boca Raton Chamber
**Industry**: BSCAI, ISSA, IICRC

### Local Partnerships
- Real estate/property management companies
- HVAC, plumbing, restoration, construction firms
- Interior designers and architects (luxury market)
- Condo associations and HOA boards

### Content-Based Link Building
- Original research: "State of Commercial Cleaning in South Florida"
- Comprehensive guides: mold remediation, marble care, hurricane prep
- Case studies on notable projects
- Infographics on cleaning best practices

### Journalist Outreach
- Miami Herald, Sun Sentinel, Palm Beach Post
- Hurricane prep/workplace hygiene expert commentary
- HARO responses
